Behind the AI Technology

How Our Models Predict Injuries

Discover the clinical decision logic and neural networks that differentiate a simple sprain from a bone fracture.

psychology

1. The Symptom Triage Model

When you answer clinical questions about pain level, swelling, bruising, and mobility loss, our backend processes the inputs using a **Random Forest Classifier** machine learning model.

1

The "Panel of Experts" concept

Imagine consulting 200 doctors. Each doctor makes a decision by asking a series of yes/no questions (a decision tree). The final diagnosis is decided by a majority vote.

2

Weighted Indicators

The model has learned that some signs (like visible bone deformity or sharp pain on bone pressure) are extremely high indicators of a fracture, while others (like general mild swelling) point towards a sprain.

Model Features Used

Pain intensity · Bruising · Obvious deformity · Loss of movement · Point tenderness · Weight-bearing capability · Mechanism of injury

radiology

2. The X-Ray Image Classifier

When you upload an X-ray scan, our system uses a **Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)**. This is a deep learning model specifically designed to read and analyze images.

1

The "Visual Filter" concept

The CNN scans the image pixel-by-pixel, applying special mathematical filters to highlight borders, textures, and lines in the bone. It checks if there are dark breaks or fractures in the highlighted edges.

2

Calibrated Probability Thresholds

A typical AI might check if the prediction is over 50%. Our model is calibrated to classify a scan as a fracture if the sigmoid value drops below the strict threshold of **0.002**, capturing even faint hairline cracks.

Technical Specifications

Input size: 128x128 pixels · Layer types: Convolutional (feature extractor) & Pooling (reducer) · Output: Sigmoid probability (fracture vs. normal)

How an Assessment Flows

1

Data Gathering

You fill out symptoms or drop an X-ray image into the triage box.

2

Preprocessing

Images are resized to 128x128 and normalized so lighting doesn't confuse the AI.

3

Model Processing

The RandomForest evaluates symptom trees; the CNN reads pixel borders.

4

Clinical Calibration

Results are translated to safety levels (e.g. Red for Fracture Emergency, Green for Sprain).

Important Medical Disclaimer

Our models are highly trained but provide **preliminary triage estimates** for guidance only. Hairline fractures can be extremely subtle, and swelling can disguise deeper tissue tears. **Never ignore clinical pain.** Always consult a certified radiologist or medical practitioner for an official diagnosis.

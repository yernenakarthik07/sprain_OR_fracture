Understanding Health Information Privacy and Security

What is HIPAA?

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) is a federal law enacted in 1996 that establishes national standards to protect sensitive patient health information from being disclosed without the patient's consent or knowledge.

HIPAA applies to covered entities (healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses) and their business associates who handle protected health information (PHI).

Key HIPAA Rules

shield Privacy Rule

Establishes national standards for protecting individuals' medical records and personal health information. It gives patients rights over their health information and sets rules and limits on who can access and receive your health data.

lock Security Rule

Requires appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and security of electronic protected health information (ePHI).

report Breach Notification Rule

Requires covered entities to notify affected individuals, HHS, and in some cases the media, of breaches of unsecured protected health information.

FractureOrSprain? & HIPAA

Our HIPAA Status

FractureOrSprain? is designed with privacy and security in mind. However, as a preliminary triage and educational tool that does not establish a doctor-patient relationship or provide formal medical diagnoses, we may not be classified as a HIPAA covered entity under certain circumstances.

Regardless of regulatory classification, we are committed to protecting your health information using industry-standard security practices and HIPAA-aligned principles.

Your Privacy Rights

Inspired by HIPAA principles, we respect your rights to:

  • Access: Request access to the information we have about you
  • Correction: Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal health information
  • Restriction: Request restrictions on how we use your data
  • Confidential Communication: Request communications through specific methods
  • Accounting: Request an accounting of disclosures of your information
report_problem

Data Breach Protocol

In the unlikely event of a data breach involving your health information, we will:

  • Notify affected individuals within 60 days of discovery
  • Provide detailed information about the breach and affected data
  • Outline steps we're taking to address the breach
  • Offer guidance on protective measures you can take
  • Report to appropriate authorities as required by law
